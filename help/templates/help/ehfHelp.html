Essential Host Factor
Essential Host Factors (EHFs) are a group of host cellular genes identified from genome-wide RNA interference (RNAi) screens, the knockdown effect of which greatly influence the infection of a pathogen but not the viability of host cells (designated as "confirmed hits" from confirmatory screen, see the workflow chart below for detailed information ). Generally, researches focus on the EHFs whose knockdown effect lead to the phenotype of totally abolished or significantly decreased pathogenic infections. In addition, EHFs can also be identified from a selected group of genes or a single gene that related to a specific phase of infection, e.g., cell entry, phagocytosis. Meanwhile, EHF data are collected from literature, of which most researchers also provide further results of functional studies to validate the essentiality of one or a small groups of top candidate genes selected from confirmed hits (designated as "primary hits" from confirmatory screen, see the workflow chart below for detailed information ). Note: when a host cellular gene is identified as an EHF, a specific pathogen (specie/strain) and screen should be designated for clarity, meaning that the definition of EHF is pathogen/screen dependent.
